[6. MITIGATING FACTORS & PLAUSIBILITY ASSESSMENT]

{{mitigating_factors}}

Mitigating factors may include:
- Confirmed financing arrangements  
- Parent guarantees  
- Binding sales contracts  
- Cost-reduction plans  
- Equity injections  
- Sale of non-core assets  

Evaluate:
- Is each factor **within management control**?  
- Is documentation binding or speculative?  
- Are assumptions realistic and evidenced?

[7. DISCLOSURE EVALUATION (ISA 570 + IAS 1)]

{{disclosure_evaluation}}

Disclosure categories:

1. **No material uncertainty** → no special disclosure required, unless voluntarily included.  
2. **Material uncertainty adequately disclosed** → EOM required.  
3. **Material uncertainty NOT adequately disclosed** → FS materially misstated → Qualified or Adverse opinion.  

Disclosure must:
- describe the uncertainty  
- specify underlying conditions  
- specify management’s plans  
- avoid ambiguous or misleading language

[9. AUDITOR’S OVERALL CONCLUSION (ISA 570)]

{{conclusion}}

Conclusion categories:

1. **No material uncertainty exists**  
   - GC assumption appropriate  
   - FS fairly presented  
   - No EOM required  

2. **Material uncertainty exists, but adequately disclosed**  
   - EOM required  
   - Opinion remains unmodified  

3. **Material uncertainty exists, disclosure inadequate**  
   - FS materially misstated  
   - Qualified or Adverse opinion  

4. **Insufficient evidence to conclude**  
   - Scope limitation  
   - Possible Disclaimer  

Opinion linkage is mandatory under ISA 700–706.
