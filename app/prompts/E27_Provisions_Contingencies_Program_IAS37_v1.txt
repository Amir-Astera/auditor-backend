[2. RISK ASSESSMENT — ISA 315 + ISA 540]

{{risk_assessment}}

Key risks:
- Understatement of provisions (common bias)  
- Failure to recognise a present obligation  
- Overly optimistic management assumptions  
- Incorrect discount rates  
- Missing legal/dispute information  
- Inadequate disclosures (ISA 701 KAM linkage possible)  

Fraud risks (ISA 240):
- Failure to record expected outflows  
- Releasing provisions inappropriately to improve profit  
- Manipulating assumptions (probability, amounts)

[5. PROVISION MEASUREMENT TESTING (IAS 37 + ISA 540)**]

{{provision_measurement}}

Provisions must be measured at:
- best estimate of outflow  
- discounted when material  
- risk-adjusted if applicable  

Steps:
- Inspect management calculations  
- Recalculate models (discounting, probability weighting)  
- Evaluate assumptions (inflation, settlement date, risk adjustments)  
- Test completeness of claims and disputes  
- Evaluate historical accuracy of estimates  
- Validate legal basis for recognition of present obligation

{{contingency_evaluation}}

  *Remote* → no disclosure  
  *Probable but unmeasurable* → disclose  

- **Contingent assets**

[7. ONEROUS CONTRACTS TESTING]

{{onerous_contracts}}

IAS 37 rule:
- A contract is onerous when unavoidable costs > economic benefits.

Procedures:
- Evaluate contract terms  
- Test estimates of unavoidable costs  
- Review forecasts of economic benefits  
- Inspect termination clauses  
- Link with impairment tests (E19 / E16)

[9. DECOMMISSIONING / ENVIRONMENTAL PROVISION (IFRS & IAS 37 LINKED TO IFRS 16)**]

{{decommissioning_review}}

Applies when:
- Obligation to restore site  
- Long-term industrial assets  
- Oil, gas, telecom, manufacturing, mining  

Procedures:
- Inspect legal/environmental obligations  
- Review discount rates and inflation assumptions  
- Recalculate present value of expected cost  
- Link to PPE (E19) → adjust ROU asset and DTL if IFRS 16 relevant
