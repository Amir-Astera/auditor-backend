This document sets out the procedures performed in identifying and assessing related party 
relationships and transactions, evaluating disclosures, and forming a conclusion on 
compliance with the applicable financial reporting framework.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[1. OBJECTIVES OF THE PROCEDURES]

In accordance with ISA 550, the objectives of the auditor are to:

1. Identify related parties and understand the nature of the relationships.
2. Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding related party transactions.
3. Determine whether related party transactions have been appropriately accounted for 
   and disclosed in the financial statements.
4. Identify undisclosed related parties or relationships.
5. Evaluate whether transactions were conducted at arm’s length (when relevant).

[4. IDENTIFICATION PROCEDURES]

AUDITOR PROCEDURES REQUIRED BY ISA 550:

1. **Obtain management’s list of related parties**  
   {{related_party_list}}

2. **Evaluate completeness of management’s list**  
   Methods include:
   - Review of shareholder registry  
   - Review of minutes of board meetings  
   - Review of KYC documents  
   - Search for common directors/owners across entities  
   - Review of large or unusual transactions  
   - Cross-check against vendor and customer master files  
   - Review of loan agreements and guarantees  

3. **Perform name-matching analytics**  
   Examples:
   - Fuzzy match of vendor/customer names against management list  
   - Identification of repeated surnames  
   - Identification of addresses or phone numbers shared with management  

4. **Review of GL for unusual related-party indicators**  
   - Accounts with "related", "interco", "affiliate", "director", "loan to/from"  
   - Manual journal entries affecting related-party accounts (link to E11)

[6. TESTING PROCEDURES (ISA 550 REQUIREMENTS)]

TESTING MATRIX:

1. **Existence / Occurrence**
   - Trace transactions to supporting documentation  
   - Inspect contracts, agreements, loan documents  
   - Confirm balances with related entities (if appropriate)

2. **Valuation**
   - Agree amounts to invoices, statements, repayment schedules  
   - For loans: recalculate interest  
   - Evaluate fairness of valuation inputs

3. **Arm’s Length Assessment**
   - Compare terms to market-based pricing  
   - Check whether similar deals exist with non-related parties  
   - Identify preferential or punitive terms

4. **Presentation & Disclosure**
   - Compare disclosures to IFRS/GAAP requirements  
   - Evaluate completeness and accuracy of related party note  
   - Assess consistency with entity’s list and accounting records

[9. FRAUD-RELATED PROCEDURES (ISA 240 LINKAGE)]

Mandatory fraud evaluation items:
- Whether any related parties are undisclosed  
- Whether transactions lack commercial rationale  
- Whether transactions seem designed to manipulate results  
- Whether related parties used for round-trip transactions  
- Whether significant adjustments affect related party accounts

[10. EVALUATION OF DISCLOSURE ADEQUACY]

Criteria:
- Completeness of relationships listed  
- Completeness of transaction disclosures  
- Accuracy of balances and terms  
- Compliance with IFRS (IAS 24)  
- Whether any material undisclosed related parties remain
