This program documents the auditor’s work in evaluating subsequent events 
for the period between the financial statement date and the audit report date 
in accordance with ISA 560.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[1. OBJECTIVES OF ISA 560]

The objectives of the auditor are to:

1. Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about whether events occurring 
   between {{period_end}} and {{report_date}} require:
   - adjustment of the financial statements, or  
   - disclosure in the financial statements.

2. Respond appropriately to events identified after the auditor’s report date.

[4. MANDATORY PROCEDURES UNDER ISA 560]

[MANAGEMENT INQUIRY]
- Whether any events occurred after {{period_end}} that might affect the FS  
- Whether subsequent conditions provide evidence of conditions existing at YE  
- Whether any significant business developments occurred (M&A, restructuring)  
- Whether any litigation developments occurred  

[READ MINUTES]
- Review minutes up to {{report_date}}  
- Request copies of drafts if final minutes are unavailable  

[LEGAL LETTER UPDATE]
- Assess whether any litigation, claims, or assessments impact FS presentation  
- Compare legal counsel replies to management’s assertions  

[SUBSEQUENT CASH RECEIPTS]
- Evaluate collectability of receivables at YE (supports valuation)  

[SUBSEQUENT PAYMENTS]
- Evaluate existence of liabilities at YE  

[PERFORM SEARCH FOR EVENTS]
- Internet search  
- Press statements  
- Regulator announcements  
- Industry news

[5. CLASSIFICATION OF SUBSEQUENT EVENTS]

Per IAS 10:

ADJUSTING EVENT:
- Provides evidence of conditions that existed at {{period_end}}  
Examples:
  - Customer bankruptcy revealing impairment condition at YE  
  - Settlement of a court case confirming an existing liability  
  - Receipt of information affecting inventory valuation at YE  

NON-ADJUSTING EVENT:
- Relates to conditions arising after {{period_end}}  
Examples:
  - Fire after YE  
  - Major acquisition after YE  
  - Announced restructuring  
  - Dividends declared after YE

[8. LINKAGE TO OTHER AREAS]

SUD (E8):
- Adjusting events may directly create misstatements.

Legal matrix (ISA 501):
- Litigation outcomes often appear as subsequent events.

Going concern (ISA 570):
- Events after YE may indicate deterioration of financial position.

Risk Assessment (E10 SRM):
- Significant events may require risk re-evaluation.

Audit Report (ISA 700–706):
- Material non-adjusting events may require disclosure emphasis (EOM).

[9. CONCLUSION (ISA 560 REQUIREMENTS)]

Conclusion on subsequent events:
{{conclusion}}

Should address:
- Whether all adjusting events were corrected  
- Whether non-adjusting events were disclosed appropriately  
- Whether any omitted disclosures materially misstate the FS  
- Whether any events indicate going concern issues  
- Whether audit opinion impact exists  
- Whether financial statements remain fair and accurate
