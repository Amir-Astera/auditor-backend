BIOLOGICAL ASSETS & AGRICULTURE — IAS 41  
Client: {{client_name}}  
Period Ended: {{period_end}}

This program covers biological assets measured at fair value less cost to sell (FVLCTS),
including plants, animals, crops, orchards, fisheries, forests, and agricultural produce.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[1. BIOLOGICAL ASSET CLASSES]

{{bio_asset_classes}}

Typical categories:
- Livestock (cattle, sheep, goats, poultry, fish)  
- Perennial plants (trees, vines)  
- Orchards / vineyards  
- Crops (cotton, wheat, corn, rice)  
- Forestry assets  
- Plantations (tea, coffee, nuts, palms)  
- Biological transformation assets (growth cycles)

[2. RISK ASSESSMENT — IAS 41]

{{risk_assessment}}

Key risks:
- Incorrect fair value measurement  
- Missing cost-to-sell adjustments  
- Unobservable inputs (Level 3)  
- Manipulation of valuation assumptions  
- Incorrect harvest recognition  
- Misclassification: biological asset vs bearer plant (IAS 16)  
- Incorrect treatment of produce (IAS 2)  

Fraud risks:
- Inflating fair value to boost profit  
- Understating costs to inflate FVLCTS  
- Creating fictitious assets (livestock, orchards, forestry areas)

[3. EXISTENCE TESTING (CRITICAL)**]

{{existence_testing}}

Tests:
- Physical inspection  
- Drone / satellite imagery (advanced audit)  
- Reconciliation to biological asset registers  
- Reconciliation to veterinary / agricultural records  
- Headcounts for livestock  
- Age-class segmentation

  asset_type: "<string>"

[4. CLASSIFICATION TESTING — IAS 41 vs IAS 16 vs IAS 2]

{{classification_testing}}

Rules:
- **Bearer plants** → IAS 16 (PPE)  
- Biological assets → IAS 41  
- Agricultural produce at harvest → IAS 2  
- Distinguish consumable vs bearer plants  

Schema:

CLASSIFICATION_SCHEMA:
  id: "<BIOCLSFxx>"
  asset_name: "<string>"
  classification: "<IAS41|IAS16|IAS2>"
  basis: "<text>"
  exception_flag: "<true|false>"

[5. FAIR VALUE MEASUREMENT (IFRS 13 LINK)**]

{{fair_value_measurement}}

Tests:
- Identify valuation technique (market approach, income approach, cost approach)  
- Verify observable inputs (Level 1 / Level 2)  
- Evaluate Level 3 assumptions:  
  * growth rates  
  * mortality  
  * yield  
  * discount rate  
  * price estimates  
- Recalculate FVLCTS  

Schema:

FAIR_VALUE_SCHEMA:
  id: "<FVxx>"
  asset_type: "<string>"
  valuation_technique: "<market|income|cost>"
  fair_value: "<numeric>"
  cost_to_sell: "<numeric>"
  FVLCTS: "<numeric>"
  level: "<1|2|3>"
  unobservable_inputs: "<text>"
  exception_flag: "<true|false>"

{{conclusion}}

2. Fair value observable & reliable

5. Exceptions immaterial  
6. Material misstatement exists
