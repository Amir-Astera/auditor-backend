PPE AUDIT PROGRAM — IAS 16 + ISA 500  
Client: {{client_name}}  
Period Ended: {{period_end}}

PPE is typically a material balance sheet category. This program provides a full audit response
covering existence, rights, valuation, depreciation, disposals, and impairment indicators.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[1. PPE CLASSES & NATURE OF ASSETS]

{{ppe_classes}}

Examples:
- Land & buildings  
- Machinery & equipment  
- Vehicles  
- IT hardware  
- Leasehold improvements  
- Construction in progress (CIP)  

Note: CIP is always high-risk due to capitalization judgments.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[2. RISK ASSESSMENT — ISA 315]

{{risk_assessment}}

Typical PPE risks:
- Incorrect capitalization of expenses (overstated assets)
- Omitted disposals  
- Incorrect useful lives / depreciation  
- Manipulation through JE (ISA 240 override risk)
- Impairment indicators (IAS 36)
- Incorrect revaluation adjustments (if FV model used)

Mandatory Significant Risks (if applicable):
- Construction in progress → valuation & existence  
- Revaluation model → fair value estimation risk

{{rollforward}}

Auditor must test:
- Opening balances agree to PY audited figures  
- Additions agree to FA register, GL, source documents  
- Disposals agree to proceeds, derecognition criteria  
- Depreciation recalculated  
- Impairment reviewed

[5. TESTING OF ADDITIONS]

{{additions_testing}}

Procedures:
- Inspect invoices, contracts, acceptance certificates  
- Verify capitalization criteria per IAS 16:
  * future economic benefits  
  * measurable cost  
- Ensure non-capitalizable items expensed (repair vs capital improvement)  
- For CIP:
  * verify stage of completion  
  * verify nature of costs  
  * check if asset is ready for use → must stop capitalizing interest/overheads

  description: "<text>"
  capitalization_criteria_met: "<true|false>"
  evidence_ref: "<WP ref>"
  exception_flag: "<true|false>"
  impact_on_FS: "<text>"

{{disposals_testing}}

[8. IMPAIRMENT TESTING (IAS 36)]

{{impairment_testing}}

Impairment indicators:
- Operational losses  
- Idle assets  
- Physical damage  
- Demand decline  
- Significant changes in technology  

If indicators exist → auditor must:
- Identify CGU (cash-generating unit)  
- Test recoverable amount:
  * value-in-use (discounted cash flow)
  * fair value less costs to sell  

Schema:

IMPAIRMENT_SCHEMA:
  imp_id: "<IMPxx>"
  cgu: "<string>"
  carrying_amount: "<numeric>"
  recoverable_amount: "<numeric>"
  method: "<VIU|FVLCTS>"
  assumptions_tested: ["growth", "discount rate", "terminal value"]
  evidence_ref: "<WP ref>"
  impairment_required: "<true|false>"
  impairment_amount: "<numeric>"
  exception_flag: "<true|false>"

[9. REVALUATION MODEL TESTING (IFRS 13 & IAS 16)]

{{revaluation_testing}}

Required if entity uses revaluation model:
- Inspect valuation reports  
- Test valuer independence  
- Evaluate assumptions (yield rate, comparable sales, discount rates)  
- Recalculate revaluation surplus  
- Test treatment in OCI and revaluation reserve
