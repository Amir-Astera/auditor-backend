{{inventory_categories}}

- Raw materials

[2. RISK ASSESSMENT — ISA 315]

{{risk_assessment}}

Key risks:
- Incorrect costing (overstated/understated inventory)  
- Incorrect overhead allocation  
- Manipulation of standard costs  
- Fictitious WIP progress (fraud indicator)  
- Incorrect treatment of abnormal costs  
- Incorrect NRV write-down reversal  

Fraud red flags:
- sudden FG margin improvement without reason  
- unexplained variances in standard costing  
- manual override in production system

[4. COSTING METHOD TESTING (IAS 2 REQUIREMENT)]

{{costing_method}}

IAS 2 allows:
- FIFO  
- Weighted Average Cost (WAC)  
- Specific identification (only for unique goods)

Auditor must:
- verify method consistency  
- recalculate FIFO layers or WAC  
- trace unit cost to purchase invoices  
- ensure no LIFO (forbidden under IFRS)  

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[5. BILL OF MATERIALS (BOM) TESTING]

{{bom_testing}}

Procedures:
- Inspect BOM for selected FG items  
- Recalculate material cost  
- Trace material cost to COGS / system costs  
- Inspect engineering change orders (ECOs)  
- Identify phantom or inactive BOM components

[6. OVERHEAD ALLOCATION TESTING]

{{overheads_testing}}

Types of overhead:
- Fixed production overhead  
- Variable overhead  
- Indirect labor  
- Utilities  
- Depreciation of production assets  

Audit procedures:
- Recalculate overhead absorption rate  
- Evaluate denominator level (normal capacity)  
- Identify abnormal costs → must be expensed (IAS 2 rule)  
- Test allocation base (labor hours, machine hours, units)  
- Recalculate overhead absorption into inventory  

Schema:

OVERHEAD_SCHEMA:
  id: "<OHxx>"
  overhead_type: "<fixed|variable>"
  rate_per_unit: "<numeric>"
  allocation_base: "<labor_hours|machine_hours|units>"
  allocation_amount: "<numeric>"
  abnormal_cost_detected: "<true|false>"
  evidence_ref: "<WP ref>"
  exception_flag: "<true|false>"

[10. NRV TESTING LINKAGE (E17)]

{{nrv_testing_link}}

NRV = selling price – selling costs  
Cross-link with E17 valuation schema (VALxx).  

Auditor must test:
- slow-moving stock  
- obsolete materials  
- selling prices  
- marketing/sales updates  
- write-down reversals (IAS 2 prohibits reversals above cost)
